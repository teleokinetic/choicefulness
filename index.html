The two-minute move

v0.1 · run it as written; wrongness is revision material

  1. Feet. Feel no separation between your feet and the floor.
  2. Settle downward inside your chest and neck. The urge pulls upward — you settle down. Sitting in your heart; no pressing, no collapse.
  3. Find the raw quality of desire in your body — wanting itself, before any object. Wherever it’s easiest.
  4. Ask: what is this urge actually asking for? Find the want inside it. Name it once, if naming helps.
  5. Feel that want directly, as raw desire. Bathe in it, no subject. Not the note that it’s there — the wanting itself, in the body.
  6. Now choose, from here. Either way, the rule is kept.

Failure modes. Toll-booth — running the move to pay for a yes; detector: nothing was felt. Grade the run, not yourself. · Noting instead of feeling — locating the want and filing the note. · Arguing with the urge — there’s no case to win. · Skipping the ground — steps 1–2 are what make 3–5 possible.
